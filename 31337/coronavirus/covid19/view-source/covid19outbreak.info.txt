      What Is the Difference Between Isolation, Containment, and Quarantine?
    </a>
  </h1>
<div class="blog-excerpt">
  <div class="blog-excerpt-wrapper"></div>
  
    <div class="blog-body-wrapper"><div class="sqs-layout sqs-grid-12 columns-12" data-layout-label="Post Body" data-type="item" data-updated-on="1584239602874" id="item-5e6d91456f379e18589a5913"><div class="row sqs-row"><div class="col sqs-col-12 span-12"><div class="sqs-block html-block sqs-block-html" data-block-type="2" id="block-62f18fbfc68fea8eccd6"><div class="sqs-block-content"><ul data-rte-list="default"><li><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Isolation in the hospital is when a patient is kept in a single, special room that has the equipment to suck air out of it (i.e., a negative pressure room). It usually has a separate adjacent room (an antechamber) that sits between the isolation room and the hallway. This allows for doctors and nurses and respiratory therapists to enter and exit without exposing the isolation room to the hallway. </p></li><li><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Isolation outside the hospital means that a person stays at home if they have concerning symptoms (fever, cough). In areas with drive-through COVID-19 testing, each persons car is effectively an isolation room. </p></li><li><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Containment is the society level strategy of cancelling large gatherings, suspending schools, and encouraging social distancing. </p></li><li><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;">Quarantine is effectively what China did in 2/2020 and what Italy is doing in 3/2020  allowing people to leave their homes only for essential travel such as medical care and obtaining groceries. </p></li><li><p class="" style="white-space:pre-wrap;"><strong>Implication</strong>: We need studies to understand the effectiveness of the above strategies on viral transmission. These studies can only be possible with direct significant government funding. </p></li></ul></div></div></div></div></div></div>
